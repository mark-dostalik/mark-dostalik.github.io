About Me

My academic background is in physics and mathematical modeling. During my PhD studies, I focused on thermomechanics of complex fluids, while during my postdoctoral research, I was developing a mathematical model for cell membrane growth in bacterial division. I have a strong foundation in mathematics, particularly in partial differential equations, as well as probability and statistics, linear algebra, and machine learning.

In between my MSc and PhD studies, I gained valuable industry experience at SpaceKnow, where I worked as a machine learning engineer in geospatial analysis. During this time, I was introduced to machine learning and developed expertise in image processing and computer vision. My role also involved general software development in Python.

In the summer of 2022, I joined the international pharmaceutical company MSD as a data scientist. My primary focus here is on developing software infrastructure for processing and analyzing image data obtained through spatial transcriptomics technologies. This innovative profiling method enables the mapping of gene expressions to spatial locations within histological tissue sections, making it particularly valuable for studies related to Alzheimer's disease.
